Personas & Storyboarding: CIT Elevators
Description Observation Interview Personas Storyboard


Description

The elevators are arguably the most frequently used facilities in the CIT. Given their almost identical designs, functions, and users, I consider the two elevators as a single, complete interface.


Observation

  • The elevators have their peak times right before and after classes.
  • Some users don't mind which elevator they will take or how long they will wait.
  • Some users call up both elevators and take the one that arrives first.
  • A group of people arrive at the elevators at the same time usually only call one elevator and take the elevator together.
  • While waiting for the elevator, some people focus on the state-showing screen while others only occasionally look up.
  • Users usually look out after they enter the cabin.
  • Users usually look up for the inner state-showing screen before they march out.
  • It usually takes some time for users to hit the desired button on the inner key pad.


    • Interview

    Which floor are users going to?

  • Floor 3 to 5.

    • Why are users going there?

  • For class, meeting, or grabing a place for study.

    • How often do users take elevators (per times they visits CIT)?

  • Depends on which floor the users usually go
    • If the destination is on the third floor or higher, Users almost always use elevators.
    • Users hardly use the elevators to reach the second floor.
  • On average, more than 50 percent of times.

    • How long have users waited for the elevator?

  • During peak times (right before and after the classes), users report a waiting time longer than 1 minute.
  • Some users do not notice or mind the waiting time at all.

    • Do users usually hit the “open door” button? Do they feel it is effective?

  • Users use the "open door" button if they are the first entering the elevator or if they see someone's heading toward the elevator.
  • The button is, and has to be, effective.

    • Do users usually hit the “close door” button? Do they feel it is effective?

  • Some users have the habit to hit the "close door" button as long as the waiting group enters the elevator. They feel the button ineffective, though.
  • Some users hit the button if they are rushing.
  • Most users hardly use the button.

    • Is there anything about the elevator not asked but users want to share?

  • It's very strange that higher levels of CIT don't have the state-showing screen.
  • The buttons on the internal key pad of the larger elevator are strangely organized.
